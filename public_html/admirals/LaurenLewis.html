Background

Yo, Lauren here. Where do I begin... I'm genderfluid, I'm polyamorous, I'm asexual! More specifically I'm genderfae(fluidly feminine, demi-girl, agender but never any masculine genders). I'm egalitarian polyamorous loving two partners equally without hierarchy. Depending on my flowing gender, I'm most often demisexual and panromantic. I am so many things.

If you see me around, feel free to stop me and say hi. That's at either at the UofA Chess Club, at the UofA Observatory, in The Shack, or around campus, anywhere really. I'm friendly and I don't bite. I love boardgames and programming and sci-fi, especially the old sci-fi classics(Battlestar Galactica, Star Trek, and Babylon 5 to name a few).

Anyways, I joined a couple years ago, just as one generation of execs were getting old and graduating. When April rolled around there was only 3 of us undergrads who qualified for exec roles, and only I was willing to handle the President Captain's responsibilities. I was really eager to be a part of something and make a difference.

Honestly, I wasn't ready to be Captain. My first year, I dropped the membership fee, didn't attend clubs fair, and din't put up posters. I had a ton of fun introducing our group to D&D, but overall our club membership declined. History shows my first year hit an all-time high for club membership in January, but I think that was a little luck plus a lot of D&D.

I've been working hard to enlarge the club and honor my predeccessors. This club has been running since the 80s and I want it to keep running for decades to come. I want to bring back SCi-Fi movie nights. I want to bring back book club. I've gotten serious about advertising for the club and running our events. History will probably remember me for running D&D.

I recently retired from being Captain/President. I'm still really active in the club. I'm really proud of breaking the all-time record for club membership again, this time due to exceptional collaboration with other clubs. I think we were at 37 active members!? I'm really proud on my successor, Ana! I bet we'll never get a president better than her.
