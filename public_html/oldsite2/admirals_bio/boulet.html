Background

After joining the club in september 2006, Joel attendeed a number of events before being elected to the position of extended executive. While holding the position of extended executive, Joel organized some events and held regular office hours as per constitutional requirments. Partway through the year, Joel was appointed to be the club's webmaster and took over responsibility of the cub's website, ualberta email address and a moderator role on the mailing list. Near the end of the year he began looking updating the website to have more accurate information. He was then elected to the position of president for the 2009-2010 school year.

Upon taking office as President, plans were put in motion to clean up the office and work began on the project over the summer of 2009. By the fall, the construction phase of the office clean-up was ready to begin. Over the fall, the club acquired a new desk, couch, shelves and built a raised floor in the 'bathroom'-closet. Joel also proposed and got passed a constitutional ammendement that brought the constitution up to date with modern club practices as well as installing new rules for on-line elections and officially updating the club's name to include the term 'fantasy'. As the school year progressed there were also a few updates to the website. As the years end approached, Joel organized and ran the club's firt online elections (not counting the constitutional ammendment) and stepped down in preparation for a 16 month internship at IBM.

While on Intership, assisted remortely with a few adminsistrative actions as well as overhauling the club website.

Upon returning to Edmonton at the close of Internship, joined President Jared in running the weekly movie night before establishing a second weekly movie night on Tusday in addition to the primary one on wednesday. At end of winter term, handed off website responsibilty to new member.
