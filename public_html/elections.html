A little bit over a year, but it's been a really enjoyable year with this club, even if there has been a pandemic happening.

What plans do you have if you get elected?

I also want to ensure that the club is a place where people can continue to be comfortable being themselves.

Why should people vote for you?

I want to help maintain the club for future classes of students to be able to enjoy in a similar way to what we've all come to enjoy. I feel like this club has presented a lot of fun opportunities for lots of people over the years, and I want to be able to proudly say that I helped contribute to tradition.

Why do you care about this club?

Once upon a time there was a first year student (or possibly plural students, three raccoons masquerading under a single CCID, but shhh) who went to clubs fair during her orientation, as one does. At this clubs fair, a certain club that starts with Sub and ends in Space was being tabled, and she had their programming quite aggressively pitched to her. She doesn't know why. Maybe it was fate, maybe she simply looked like an easy pitching mark. Out of sheer intimidation, she signed up for their emailing list. It has been just under 2 years, 1 pandemic, and 4.5 D&D campaigns since then, and she's still here. One would suspect it's no longer because of intimidation. In fact, one could quite safely say that she likes this whole science fiction and fantasy thing, she's made friends here, and generally considers this a pretty chill space.

How long have you been in the club?

Since September 2019

What makes you a good candidate?

As not one but three raccoons, I bring to the table three times the opposable thumbs of your average candidate, for three times the productivity. Also possibly three times the, uh, loose fur and a distinct fear of large owls, dogs and loud noises, but that's beyond the point. I may be inexperienced at this whole ... bipedal walking thing, but rest assured one does not need to be passably human to have an appreciation for the wonderous pop culture humans have created. I consume science fiction and fantasy books at an alarming rate (and by that I mean I read them, I don't eat them! I may be a procyonid but I'm not a savage!), know a decent amount about D&D, and have three times the normal human amount of enthusiasm for learning more.
