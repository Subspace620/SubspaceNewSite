Admiral Malmot

Real Name: Tom Lam
Club Position: Former President (1996-1997)
Service Record:
Joined club, Sept. 1994.
Elected secretary/treasurer, March 1994.
Officially assumed executive position Sept. 1994; promoted to Lt. Cmdr.
Elected club president, March 1995.
Officially assumed presidency Sept. 1995; promoted to Captain.
Elected vice-president, March 1996; assumed executive position Sept. 1996.
Retired from executive service, March 1997; promoted to Admiral.
The Federation was handed a bitter pill to swallow recently, when Admiral Malmot, one of Starfleet's most distinguished officers, announced that he was actually a RomuLam double agent. The announcement put to rest the long months of specualtion concerning the supposedly Vulcan's mysterious disappearance during a routine survey mission on Hrconus III.

Malmot, now a praetor of the Star Empire, served a long, fruitful career in Starfleet, including full tours of duty as second officer aboard the U.S.S. Incontinent, and as the captain of the U.S.S. Inconceivable. The RomuLam agent occasionally appears in the Sol Univas B system to harass Captain Robert Leddy and the crew of SubSpace 6-20. Captain Leddy believes these visitations underlie Malmot's resentment toward the station commander.

"It was pretty clear from the start that he couldn't tolerate taking orders from someone with no experience as a starship captain," commented Leddy, on the Inconceivable's reassignment to SubSpace 6-20. When asked about the repeated appearances of Malmot's ship, the Warbird Peregrine, Leddy speculated that "he probably does it just to play mind games with our command staff.
